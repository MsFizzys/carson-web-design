My Story

Rooted in Marin.
Driven by Purpose.

I'm Carson Dohemann — born and raised in Marin County, California. Growing up here was a dream and a huge influence in me having an active lifestyle: snowboarding, surfing, hiking etc. Before web design, I spent years working as a plumber in San Francisco with Local 38, a trade that taught me discipline, problem-solving, and the satisfaction of building something that works exactly the way it should.

In 2019, my life changed in an instant. I broke my vertebrae diving into the water at the beach, an injury that affected my spinal cord and left me paralyzed and in a wheelchair. There's no clean way to describe what that transition feels like — losing the sports you love, rebuilding your identity from the ground up, and learning to find meaning in a life that looks completely different than the one you'd planned. It takes time. It takes honesty. And it takes the right people beside you.

My wife has been everything through that journey. Together, we made a choice not to wait for life to return to what it was, but to build something new with what we have. We launched two businesses: Made in Marin, a clothing brand rooted in the landscape and culture of the county we call home, and Ms. Fizzy's, Marin County's first dirty soda catering company, bringing handcrafted sodas and iced coffees to events across the North Bay.

It was building the website for Ms. Fizzy's that lit the spark. I discovered that I had a real passion for web design — the creativity of it, the strategy behind it, and most importantly, the results it produces. I watched firsthand how a well-built, properly optimized website could open doors and bring in customers who would have never found us otherwise.

Since then, I've been designing websites for other businesses in my spare time. A thoughtfully built, SEO-forward website is one of the most powerful investments a small business can make. Web design has become more than work for me — it has filled a space that snowboarding and surfing once occupied, and it gives me a way to channel that same drive and competitive spirit into something that genuinely helps people.

If you're ready to build a website that actually works for your business — one that shows up in search, earns trust, and turns visitors into customers — I'd love to be the one to build it with you.
